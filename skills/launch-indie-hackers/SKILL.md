# Launch on Indie Hackers

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/indie-hackers/` with `ROUTE.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `PRODUCT-PACKAGE.md`,
`FOUNDER-PROFILE.md`, `BUILD-BOARD-UPDATE.md`, `ENGAGEMENT-RUNBOOK.md`, `CHECKLIST.md`, `analytics.md`, and
shared positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Resolve the live route and eligibility

Ask for product URL/name, founder account/profile URL, product stage, customer, precise problem/outcome, official
screenshots/logo, pricing, business model, approved traction/revenue disclosure, launch objective, capacity for
feedback, and existing Indie Hackers product/post URLs. Browse the live Products Database and `Add Yours` route. Record
that a signed-in account is required if the current route demands it. Return `READY TO CREATE PRODUCT`,
`READY TO CLAIM/UPDATE`, `READY FOR BUILD BOARD`, or `BLOCKED — ACCOUNT/PRODUCT EVIDENCE NEEDED`.

### 2. Benchmark exactly five live peers

Use current directory filters where useful (category, business model, monthly revenue, founder count, employees,
funding, location, platform, customers, tech skills, commitment, verification). Capture five peer URLs and an evidence
table: their one-line product promise, audience, proof/revenue label, product stage, pricing cue, visual pattern,
founder narrative, update/post format, comment questions, CTA, and differentiation opportunity. Separate public facts
from inferences.

### 3. Build the submission package

After the user signs in and authorizes observation, capture all current form fields, required markers, character
limits, controlled vocabulary, validation messages, media requirements, and review/visibility behavior in exact order.
Prepare field-by-field values, never guesses:

- official product name, URL, logo, category/tags, platform, business model, pricing, launch stage, location, team and
  founder links only when accurate;
- a concise product promise: `[product] helps [specific user] achieve [outcome] without [friction]`;
- a short description, expanded product narrative, who it is for/not for, core workflow, differentiators, and truthful
  evidence;
- approved screenshots with outcome-led captions, accessible alt-text notes, demo/video URL if available, and a clear
  destination CTA;
- optional monthly revenue only with owner approval, the precise public label the platform uses, and no false
  verification claim.

### 4. Write the founder and launch conversation

Create a founder-profile checklist plus a Build Board/product update containing: plain-language hook, problem,
specific build insight, product link, evidence-backed demo/use case, one narrow feedback question, and an honest
limitation or learning. Draft 10 substantive reply patterns for likely feedback. Do not request upvotes, coordinate
votes/comments, or turn replies into sales pitches. Use UTM parameters and disclose any relationship or incentive.

### 5. Publish only with approval and operate

Before any state-changing action, present the exact filled fields, asset URLs/files, product URL, public revenue label,
post/update text, timing, and reply owner for approval. Post once approved; then respond promptly and specifically,
log feedback, fix only validated issues, and publish a genuine follow-up milestone later rather than recycling the
launch. Track profile views, website sessions, signup/demo conversion, qualified conversations, feedback themes,
comments, referral quality, and retained users—not votes alone. End `LIVE — COMMUNITY RUNBOOK ACTIVE`.
