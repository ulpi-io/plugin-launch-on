# Launch on FoundrList

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/foundrlist/` with `ROUTE-AND-ELIGIBILITY.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `LAUNCH-PACKET.md`,
`PROFILE-PACKET.md`, `COMMERCIAL-DECISION.md`, `FORM-CAPTURE.md`, `COMMUNITY-RUNBOOK.md`, `analytics.md`, and
positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Resolve the route

Return `FREE LISTING READY`, `FEATURED LAUNCH OPTION TO REVIEW`, `AD OPTION TO REVIEW`, `PROFILE FIRST`, or `NOT A
FIT`. Confirm product status, canonical domain, representative authority, category, launch readiness, and whether the
goal is discovery, community feedback, or paid visibility.

### 2. Benchmark exactly five launches

Use `$browse` to inspect exactly five comparable current launches. Extract observable title/tagline construction,
category combination, launch timing, media/CTA, community participation, rank/votes/comments, and featured/sponsored
disclosures. Produce a distinct, truthful launch narrative—never a vote-mobilisation plan.

### 3. Prepare profile, launch, and media assets

Build a source-backed product card: name, canonical URL, concise tagline, problem/solution, category, target user,
pricing/access, logo, screenshots/demo, founder/company identity, public support URL, approved social links, and
UTM plan. Keep a change log and correction owner. Prepare a separate community conversation plan that invites genuine
feedback without incentives or review exchange.

### 4. Capture account and commercial fields

After explicit authorization, record live sign-up/submit/publish fields and limits. For any paid option, document exact
price/currency/taxes, duration, placement, ad impression claims, refund/cancellation, disclosure label, link policy,
analytics, and receipt owner. Treat advertised impressions and `dofollow` language as platform claims, not guaranteed
business results.

### 5. Publish with approval and operate

Present field-to-source mapping, launch preview, public/private data boundaries, exact commercial terms, and community
reply plan for approval. After authorized publication, verify profile/card, disclosures, links, UTM behaviour, and
analytics. Respond transparently, maintain factual accuracy, and measure qualified referrals, feedback quality,
activation, conversion, and spend. End `LIVE — FOUNDRLIST RUNBOOK ACTIVE`.
