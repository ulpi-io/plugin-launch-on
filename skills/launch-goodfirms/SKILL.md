<EXTREMELY-IMPORTANT>
1. CHOOSE THE CORRECT ROUTE. GoodFirms has distinct software and service surfaces. A product-only SaaS uses the
   software route; an agency/consultancy/implementation company uses the services route; a hybrid uses each route
   only for what is genuinely offered. Never turn a service into a fake software product or duplicate a profile.
2. SEARCH AND BROWSE EXACTLY FIVE PEERS. Search exact/variant names and existing profiles first. Before category,
   copy, review, or pricing choices, browse the relevant category, exactly five comparable live profiles, official
   sites, and a relevant comparison/list. Use `$browse-stealth` and headed Camoufox on challenges. If blocked after
   visual Turnstile interaction plus permitted handoff/proxy, record the live gate as blocked; do not invent evidence.
3. EXPECT VETTING, NOT GUARANTEED LISTING. The live public site reports review/application checks, portfolio/client
   evidence, weekly ranking recomputation, and a stated acceptance rate. Capture the current application fields and
   requirements; do not promise acceptance, ranking, leads, badges, or review publication.
4. PROVE EVERY PUBLIC CLAIM. Company facts, services, software features, industries, rates/pricing, locations,
   team size, portfolio/case studies, customers, integrations, security, media, and availability need first-party
   evidence and permission. Never claim the marketplace’s statistics as the vendor’s own.
5. NEVER MANIPULATE REVIEWS OR RANKING. Do not fabricate, buy, ghostwrite, gate, pressure, coordinate, or condition
   client reviews, votes, testimonials, projects, or rankings. Never impersonate a client. Use only GoodFirms’
   approved verification workflow.
6. SEPARATE ORGANIC AND PAID. GoodFirms labels sponsorship/Pro as distinct from algorithmic ranking. Verify current
   cost, placement, label, duration, terms, and metrics before recommending paid participation; never imply a paid
   placement is organic.
7. NO EXTERNAL ACTION WITHOUT APPROVAL. Never register, submit/claim/edit, publish a portfolio, contact clients,
   respond to a review, buy sponsorship, or disclose private information without explicit user approval.
</EXTREMELY-IMPORTANT>

# Launch on GoodFirms

## Deliverables

Write `.ulpi/launch/goodfirms/` with `ROUTE-AND-ELIGIBILITY.md`, `SUBMISSION.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`,
`CONTENT-REQUIREMENTS.md`, `PROFILE.md`, `PORTFOLIO-OR-PRODUCT.md`, `REVIEWS-AND-RANKING.md`, `OPERATIONS.md`,
`CHECKLIST.md`, `analytics.md`, and shared positioning.

## 1. Determine profile mode and source truth

Ask for canonical company/product URLs, whether the offer is software, services, or hybrid, primary buyer/job,
target category, pricing/rates, locations, team size, verified portfolio/customer permissions, approved media,
existing GoodFirms URL, and authorized owner. Search all name/domain variants and choose `NEW SOFTWARE`, `NEW
SERVICE`, `HYBRID`, `CLAIM/UPDATE`, `CORRECT/MERGE`, or `NOT READY`.

Capture the live Get Listed/application flow and every post-login field immediately before submission. Browse source
material—product, pricing, docs, security, integration directory, service pages, case studies, legal pages, and
assets—and retain a source/date ledger.

## 2. Benchmark exactly five category peers

Read the category definition, visible ranking/order/filter/sponsorship labels, review density, and methodology
language. Inspect exactly five comparable profiles and official sites; service peers need portfolio, location,
team/rate, industry, engagement, and client-evidence patterns, while software peers need features, integrations,
deployment, pricing/trial, use cases, media, and reviews. Read a comparison/list page. Classify each element as
`LIVE-FORM`, `CATEGORY BASELINE`, `RECOMMENDED`, `GOODFIRMS/CLIENT-GENERATED`, `SPONSORED/PRO`, or `NOT APPLICABLE`.

## 3. Build the profile package

Prepare paste-ready, evidence-backed values for identity, canonical CTA, buyer-job statement, full overview,
categories, specialties/features, industries, locations/regions, company facts, pricing/rates or pricing model,
team/availability, deployment/platform/support/security/integrations, approved logo/screenshots/video, and
source-backed FAQ. Service route: only publish consented case studies/portfolio with challenge, work performed,
outcome, scope, dates, and evidence. Software route: include only real plans/trials/feature claims and technical
compatibility. Draft three truthful angles and recommend the one most differentiated from the five peers.

## 4. Run reviews, ranking, sponsorship, and operations fairly

Use a neutral post-use, universal eligibility rule with full cohort, delay, frequency cap, reminder, suppression,
privacy basis, and audit owner. Invite honest, detailed client experience—not a positive rating. Publicly respond as
the vendor, protect PII, and avoid competitor attacks. Treat application screening, review verification, client
relationship checks, algorithmic rank, badges, research inclusion, sponsorship, and Pro as platform-controlled;
verify current entitlement before action.

Measure profile/category/list referrals, CTA-to-qualified-lead/demo conversion, portfolio engagement, approved review
recency, response SLA, profile freshness, organic vs sponsored referrals, and downstream activation via privacy-safe
UTMs. Final verdict: `READY TO APPLY`, `READY TO CLAIM/UPDATE`, `LIVE — OPERATIONS ACTIVE`, or `NOT READY — BLOCKERS REMAIN`.
