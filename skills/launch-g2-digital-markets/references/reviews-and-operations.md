## Review eligibility

Reviews must come directly from identifiable, verified, actual users and reflect their own first-hand
experience. Reviewers must write original, honest, meaningful content without automation or third-party
authorship.

Exclude:

- vendor employees, officers, directors, agents, subsidiaries, family, and other affiliates
- direct competitors or people with a financial interest in the product
- people who did not use the product
- identities that cannot be verified
- anyone asked to submit on another person's behalf

Protect privacy. Invitations should warn reviewers not to disclose personal information, confidential data,
customer records, credentials, or information covered by an NDA.

## Prohibited practices

Never:

- review-gate or select only promoters, high-NPS customers, or known positive users
- ask for five stars or a favorable review
- coach topics, wording, pros/cons, or scores
- draft, edit, translate, or submit a review for a user
- use generative AI to create review content
- commission, buy, or facilitate fake reviews
- ask employees to review their own product or competitors
- use a third-party bulk review generator
- suppress, pressure, harass, or threaten a reviewer
- condition service, discounts, or rewards on positive sentiment
- abuse reporting to remove legitimate negative feedback

Solicit a broad cross-section of users across plan, tenure, role, use case, company size, geography, and
experience. Use the same neutral request regardless of expected sentiment.

## Incentivized campaigns

Capterra's current Community Guidelines permit only compliant nominal incentives through approved processes.
Before any incentive campaign:

- use the incentive link provided in the Vendor Portal
- offer the incentive equally regardless of rating or sentiment
- keep it nominal and lawful
- disclose the incentive and material connection
- accept all verified reviews that comply with the rules
- never offer a second incentive for the same review
- honor the platform's limit on unique published reviewers
- ask Capterra for written approval before any alternative arrangement

Do not incentivize vendor affiliates, direct competitors, government/public-sector employees, people whose
employer policy prohibits gifts, sanctioned people/organizations, or anyone already incentivized for that
review.

Mandatory outreach disclosures currently include:

1. Participation is subject to Capterra's Community Guidelines.
2. The incentive is not contingent on a positive review; honest and unbiased feedback is required.
3. Reviews may appear on affiliated sites including Capterra, Software Advice, GetApp, and partners.
4. The vendor is independent from the review platform; participation does not imply platform affiliation.

Verify exact language and incentive rules immediately before launch.

## Outreach templates

### Neutral review request

~~~text
Subject: Share your experience with [Product] on Capterra

You are invited to share an honest review of your first-hand experience using [Product]. Positive, neutral,
and critical feedback are equally welcome. Please write the review in your own words and do not include
confidential or personal information.

[Official Capterra review link]
~~~

### Neutral reminder

~~~text
Subject: Reminder: optional Capterra review for [Product]

If you would like to share your first-hand experience with [Product], the Capterra review form remains
available here: [Official Capterra review link]. Participation is optional, and honest feedback of any rating
is welcome. No action is needed if you prefer not to participate.
~~~

For an incentivized campaign, append all current mandatory disclosures and use only the approved portal link.
Do not combine a review request with renewal pressure, support escalation, or a request to change criticism.

## Review responses

Vendor responses should use the Vendor Portal response feature and remain respectful, factual, and useful.

For positive reviews:

- thank the reviewer
- reference one specific, non-sensitive aspect of their experience
- avoid upselling or asking them to change the rating

For critical reviews:

- acknowledge the experience without arguing
- clarify a factual issue only when necessary
- state a concrete remediation or support path without exposing account details
- move private troubleshooting to a secure channel
- do not ask for removal or revision

For suspected violations, preserve evidence and use the official reporting process. Disagreement with an
opinion is not a violation, and reporting does not guarantee removal.

## Sponsored placement and research

Capterra offers free and paid provider services. Sponsored profiles can appear before other profiles and are
identified by link-out buttons or sponsorship treatment. Keep these concepts separate:

- paid referral traffic or sales opportunities
- organic category browsing and user-selected sorting/filtering
- independent editorial/research methodologies
- badges and report placements

Do not describe sponsored position as objective category leadership. Do not promise that spending will change
research placement. Capterra's Shortlist methodology currently considers recent user ratings and popularity
signals and normalizes scores; it is not controlled by this skill.

## 30/60/90-day plan

### Days 0–30

- resolve profile approval and category questions
- verify public rendering, canonical URL, and ownership
- complete product details, pricing, integrations, and media
- validate UTM and conversion events
- invite a broad first cohort of eligible users
- respond to every publishable review professionally

### Days 31–60

- compare profile questions with sales/support objections
- improve weak or ambiguous copy without changing category truth
- refresh screenshots if the product UI changed
- continue neutral review requests across representative segments
- measure referral quality and conversion, not raw clicks alone

### Days 61–90

- analyze published-review coverage and recency
- route recurring feedback into product and customer-success work
- assess category fit and request corrections when evidence supports them
- evaluate sponsored services separately against qualified pipeline economics
- document decisions and next-quarter owners

## Metrics and maintenance

Track controllable signals:

- profile completeness and last verification date
- approval, ownership, and category status
- number of eligible invitations and published verified reviews
- review mix by customer segment and use case
- review response time and unresolved themes
- Capterra referral sessions and landing-page engagement
- demo, trial, signup, and purchase conversions
- qualified opportunities, pipeline, and revenue when attributable
- pricing, integrations, screenshots, and product-detail freshness

Do not optimize solely for average rating or review count. A representative, current review base and an
accurate buyer-facing profile are more durable than manipulated volume.

Quarterly, recheck:

- current Capterra Profile and Community Guidelines
- live form fields and limits
- category and sponsorship presentation
- product name, URL, pricing, and availability
- languages, countries, deployment, support, and training
- integrations and category evidence
- screenshots/video and UI freshness
- review link, response queue, and analytics
