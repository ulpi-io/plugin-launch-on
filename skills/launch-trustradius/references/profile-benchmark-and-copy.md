# TrustRadius Profile Benchmark and Copy

## Top-five method

Use the narrowest live category and exactly five relevant leading profiles. Record filters/order, date/timezone,
locale, positions, URLs, and selection rationale. TrustRadius's live Employee Recognition page on July 23, 2026
stated rankings were based purely on reviews and profile completeness, with no paid placement or analyst
influence. Re-verify this promise in the target category.

The live first five in that category were Workhuman, Vantage Circle, Paylocity, Bonusly, and Guusto. This is an
example evidence set, not a universal list. Strong profile patterns included:

- concise `What is ...?` buyer-oriented overview
- multiple accurate Categories & Use Cases
- pricing/trial/demo labels and a clear CTA
- captioned screenshot galleries and video/demo content
- named integrations
- features when category data supports them
- technical deployment/mobile details, competitors, awards, and FAQs
- review-derived pros/cons, use cases, ROI, usability, alternatives, and user/company context
- buyer/installation data and AI insights controlled by TrustRadius

## Requirement classes

- `REQUIRED/BASELINE`: identity, description, correct category, canonical destination, complete core facts
- `RECOMMENDED`: repeated useful conversion element across relevant strong profiles
- `FREE`: currently editable on a claimed Free profile
- `PREMIUM/PLAN-GATED`: contract-dependent narrative, demand/intent, lead, campaign, or content capability
- `TRUSTRADIUS-GENERATED`: score, rank, awards, AI insights, review themes, usage/install data
- `NOT RELEVANT`: visible on a peer but untruthful or unhelpful for this product

## Copy

Draft three original descriptions:

1. buyer job: buyer, problem, core workflow, outcome
2. differentiation: category table stakes plus specific unique mechanisms
3. proof/reassurance: implementation, integrations, security, support, and verifiable proof

Score factuality, clarity, category fit, specificity, buyer objections, scanability, and consistency. Do not copy
competitors or reviews. Treat review-derived language as research themes, not vendor claims.

## Media and technical content

Build a narrative gallery: overview, core action, differentiated workflow, admin/reporting, integration, outcome.
Captions must say what is shown and why it matters. Verify crop, legibility, rights, freshness, mobile/desktop,
and remove PII, secrets, internal URLs, fake metrics, and unreleased functionality.

Map every category feature, integration, API, deployment type, OS/mobile app, security claim, and competitor to
first-party evidence. Mark unsupported, roadmap-only, custom-service-dependent, or expired claims `FAIL`.
