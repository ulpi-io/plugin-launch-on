# Launch on SideProjectors

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/sideprojectors/` with `ROUTE-AND-READINESS.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `PROJECT-PACKET.md`,
`METRICS-AND-PRICE.md`, `MEDIA-PLAN.md`, `FORM-CAPTURE.md`, `REVIEW-AND-MESSAGING-RUNBOOK.md`, `analytics.md`, and
positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Decide whether SideProjectors and its route fit

Return `SHOWCASE READY`, `SALE LISTING READY`, `COFOUNDER LISTING READY`, `ACCOUNT/FIELD CAPTURE NEEDED`, or `NOT
READY`. Confirm the owner has authority, the product is live or honestly described, the route expresses the real
intent, the destination URL works, and a responsible owner can answer inbound questions. Default a conventional
startup launch to `Showcase`, not `For sale`.

### 2. Benchmark exactly five live projects

Use `$browse` on the current marketplace and exactly five comparable project pages. Capture their route label, title,
short description, type, target-market framing, asset mix, public metrics, build detail, owner credibility, CTA, and
date. Build a differentiating evidence matrix. Public listings expose `Project info`, `Images & videos`, `Project
owner`, `How project was built`, and `Project metrics`; use those as a completeness benchmark, not a claim that every
field is required.

### 3. Build the factual project packet

Prepare one approved source for each claim: canonical name, project type, public URL, concise value proposition,
customer/problem/outcome, feature proof, category/target market, status/availability, founder or company identity,
support/contact path, and analytics URL. Include a clear long description, what the product does, who it serves,
what differentiates it, and what specific feedback is useful.

For the public project sections, prepare: a headline and one-line summary; logo/cover and screenshots; demo video
when available; project description; build story and technology/service detail; owner profile; and only substantiated
revenue, downloads, visitors, hours-per-week, or other metrics. Use `not disclosed` or omit a field where the
current form permits it; do not round up, imply a metric, or disclose confidential data.

### 4. Capture the live submission form after authorization

Create the account only with approval, then enter `/submit/type` and capture the chosen route plus all live fields.
Map each field to an approved source, including project title/URL, project type, target market, description,
screenshots/video, pricing/sale terms where relevant, revenue/traffic/user metrics, tech stack, owner identity,
feedback goal, consent, priority-review/featured option, validation, and submit action. Confirm image/video formats,
sizes, required captions, and any plan/price/tax/refund terms before recommending paid visibility.

### 5. Submit, review, and operate responsibly

Present the completed field map, preview, disclosures, priority-review terms (if any), and exact go-live action for
approval. After authorized submission, retain the submission timestamp and status. If approved, verify the public
route label, title, summary, images, target link/UTMs, project owner, facts, and contact path. Reply accurately to
genuine questions without pressure or incentives, correct material facts promptly, and measure qualified visits,
signups, product feedback, inquiries, conversion, and any paid spend. End `LIVE — SIDEPROJECTORS RUNBOOK ACTIVE`.
