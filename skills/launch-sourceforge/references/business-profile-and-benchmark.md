## 1. Mandatory browser research

Use `$browse` against the live relevant category, five product profiles, and each vendor's current first-party
site. On a 403/challenge, load `$browse-stealth`, verify Camoufox, stop the normal server, and retry in headed
Camoufox. If stealth is also blocked, do not finalize: document the block and use an approved user handoff or
residential proxy. Indexed snippets may guide URLs but do not replace the live five-profile gate.

Capture locale, date/timezone, category URL, filters, sort, and displayed position. SourceForge category pages
may offer Sponsored, Highest Rated, Most Reviews, or other orderings. Separate Sponsored/Partner/promoted entries
from organic results and label the selection method.

## 2. Top-five selection

Choose exactly five products a buyer would reasonably compare for the same core job. Prefer the narrow primary
category and a buyer-relevant organic sort. If there are fewer than five, broaden one level and explain why.

For every product record:

- product/vendor/profile URL, position, paid/partner/badge state, category
- audience and description structure
- pricing, trial/free version, CTA
- categories, features, deployment/platforms, organization size/type, regions/languages
- API and integrations, support and training
- logo, cover, screenshots/captions, video, downloadable assets, demos, case studies/articles
- FAQs, rating/review volume and recency, response behavior
- first-party evidence consistency and credible whitespace

Do not copy prose or reviews. Summarize patterns and buyer questions.

## 3. Public profile surfaces

Current official Product Page documentation observed July 23, 2026 includes:

- logo and optional 1300x200 cover photo
- full and category-specific descriptions
- audience, homepage URL, YouTube video, company information
- pricing, free trial/version
- support and training options
- platforms/OS/deployment
- API and integrations
- regions, competitors, categories, and category features
- organization sizes/types and languages
- up to six screenshots with captions
- generated FAQs from software details

Possible plan-gated enhancements include custom FAQs, extra category/competitor promotion, custom cover,
downloadable assets, interactive demo, visitor/buyer-intent data, and other demand-generation surfaces. Verify the
current plan before promising or drafting a gated element.

## 4. Copy and media

Write for the primary buyer job: audience/problem, what the product enables, how the core workflow works,
specific differentiators, proof/reassurance, then next action. Draft buyer-job, differentiation, and
proof/reassurance versions; score factuality, clarity, category fit, specificity, objection handling,
scanability, and first-party consistency.

Media should tell a workflow: entry point, core action, differentiated capability, administration/reporting,
and outcome. Each caption must identify what is shown and why it matters. Remove secrets, PII, test accounts,
internal URLs, unsupported platforms, and unreleased features. Confirm rights and preview crops/mobile rendering.

## 5. Category and feature evidence

Business directory filters currently expose combinations of features, categories, deployment/platform,
training, support, trial/version, integrations, API, organization types/sizes, regions, and languages. Select a
field only when a buyer can use it today. Link each category/feature to a first-party source and assign:

- `PASS` — native and central/relevant
- `BORDERLINE` — truthful but needs SourceForge review or clearer evidence
- `FAIL` — absent, roadmap-only, dependent on custom work, or misleading

Competitors must solve the same primary job for a similar buyer. Do not list famous products merely for traffic.
