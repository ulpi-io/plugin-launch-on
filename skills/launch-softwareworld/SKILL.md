# Launch on SoftwareWorld

## Deliverables

Write `.ulpi/launch/softwareworld/` containing `ROUTE-AND-CLAIM.md`, `SUBMISSION.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`,
`CONTENT-REQUIREMENTS.md`, `PROFILE.md`, `PRICING-AND-FEATURES.md`, `CATEGORY-AND-BUYER-FIT.md`,
`REVIEWS-AND-OPERATIONS.md`, `CHECKLIST.md`, `analytics.md`, and shared `.ulpi/launch/positioning.md`.

## 1. Identify route and ground source truth

Ask for canonical product/vendor URLs, intended category, buyer/job, pricing/trial/demo URLs, platforms/deployment,
languages/regions, integrations/API, approved media, existing SoftwareWorld URL, and authorized owner. Browse the
product site, docs, pricing, support/security/legal pages, integration directory, and brand assets. Output `NEW
PAID LISTING`, `CLAIM EXISTING PROFILE`, `CORRECT/MERGE`, `OPTIMIZE`, or `NOT READY` with evidence.

Use the current public Get Listed route only to capture form and policy facts; account creation or payment needs
approval. After authenticated vendor access, capture all profile fields in exact live order and label each `READY TO
PASTE`, `SELECT`, `UPLOAD`, `NOT APPLICABLE`, `NEEDS OWNER`, or `BLOCKED`.

## 2. Benchmark exactly five peers

Record category definition, filters/order, visible product count, transparency/sponsorship notices, and date. Inspect
exactly five relevant product pages and official sites. Capture overview/value statement, use cases/best-for, key
features, integrations, pricing/trial/CTA, screenshots/video, contact and company details, support/training,
licensing/deployment, target sizes, languages, industries, reviews, FAQs, alternatives/comparisons, and visible
verified/recommended/sponsored/rating/rank signals. Summarize buyer questions and gaps—never duplicate peer copy.

Classify each surface `LIVE-FORM/REQUIRED`, `CATEGORY BASELINE`, `RECOMMENDED`,
`SOFTWAREWORLD/COMMUNITY-GENERATED`, `SPONSORED/PROGRAM-GATED`, or `NOT APPLICABLE`.

## 3. Build complete profile content

Prepare evidence-backed, paste-ready values for:

- product/vendor name, canonical website/CTA, concise buyer-job line and full overview
- primary/secondary categories, business problem, use cases, best-for company sizes/industries and category rationale
- feature mapping and integrations, API, support methods, training options, licensing, deployment, native platforms,
  languages, regional constraints, security/compliance only where public proof exists
- pricing type, preferred currency, free trial/free version state, payment frequency, plan names, price/unit/inclusions,
  official pricing/trial/demo URLs, source, and verification date
- logo, screenshots, YouTube video where relevant, captions, asset rights, dimensions, freshness, and PII/secret review
- vendor contact details: vendor name, founded year, location, employee range, and source; never fabricate them
- source-backed FAQs; three copy angles (buyer outcome, workflow/implementation, proof/reassurance) and recommended
  factual version

## 4. Reviews, sponsorship, and operations

Use a universal, neutral post-use review invitation rule for the full eligible cohort, with delay, frequency cap,
reminder, suppression, privacy basis, and audit owner. Request an honest experience, not a rating or positive review.
Public responses identify vendor affiliation, protect PII, link evidence, and avoid competitor attacks.

Treat sponsorship, recommended badges, review-team content, rankings, and placement as independent platform outputs.
Verify current program scope, price, labels, duration, and reporting before purchase. Measure profile/category/
comparison referrals, CTA-to-trial/demo/docs conversion, pricing freshness, review volume/recency, response SLA, and
qualified activation with privacy-safe UTMs and first-party events.

## 5. Validate

Confirm duplicate decision, exactly-five live evidence, current account/listing requirements, field/limit/fee capture,
source ledger, category and buyer fit, accurate commercial details, asset safety, review fairness, program separation,
private-data exclusion, and human approval. Final verdict: `READY TO REGISTER AND SUBMIT`, `READY TO CLAIM/UPDATE`,
`LIVE — OPERATIONS ACTIVE`, or `NOT READY — BLOCKERS REMAIN`.
