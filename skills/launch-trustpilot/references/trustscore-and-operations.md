## 1. TrustScore interpretation

Trustpilot describes TrustScore as an overall measure of reviewer satisfaction based on service and location
reviews. Its calculation considers:

- time span/recency
- frequency
- a Bayesian average that stabilizes scores with limited data

Therefore:

- do not calculate TrustScore as a raw arithmetic average
- newer feedback can carry different practical influence from old feedback
- review volume and consistency matter to stability
- no operational plan can guarantee a future score
- product reviews may be displayed/used differently; verify the current product when relevant

Record TrustScore, star label, review count, and timestamp as observations. Do not claim that paying Trustpilot
improves the organic score.

## 2. Category position

Category pages are market/country and taxonomy dependent. Eligibility, ordering, and labels can change. Record:

- exact category URL and locale/country
- visible filters/order
- current position and timestamp
- profile category assignment
- review count/TrustScore shown at observation
- paid/promoted placements separately

Do not guarantee rank, use an irrelevant category, or advertise internal competitor benchmarks without confirming
Trustpilot's permitted-use terms. Trustpilot retains final control of classification and display.

## 4. Measurement model

Baseline before changes:

- claimed/unclaimed state and profile completeness
- TrustScore, star label, total reviews, recent-review count, and distribution
- category/market position
- invitation eligibility, sends, delivery, and review conversion
- reply coverage and median/percentile response time
- unresolved critical reviews/themes
- profile views, website referrals, and conversions where available

Leading indicators:

- genuine eligible experiences
- fair invitation coverage = invited eligible / total eligible
- delivery and bounce rate
- opt-out/complaint rate
- review conversion, never targeted rating conversion
- reply coverage and SLA attainment
- issue closure and recurrence

Outcomes:

- review freshness and volume
- TrustScore/star label and distribution
- category position
- branded search visibility
- Trustpilot referral sessions and qualified conversion
- customer-experience issue reduction

Do not optimize only for five-star share. A compliant program seeks representative feedback and better customer
experience.

## 5. Review-to-improvement loop

Weekly, aggregate themes without reviewer PII:

| Theme | Sentiment | Frequency | Severity | Evidence period | Owner | Action | Due | Status |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|---|---|---|

Connect repeated issues to product, billing, onboarding, delivery, support, policy, or expectation-setting work.
Verify closure by measuring recurrence, operational metrics, and subsequent unbiased feedback—not by pressuring
the original reviewer.

## 6. 30/60/90-day operations

Days 0–30:

- claim/activate the correct profile and resolve duplicates/access
- complete factual profile, categories, contacts, locations, and assets
- record baseline and plan entitlements
- approve neutral invitation policy, population query, privacy, suppression, and QA
- establish response owners, SLAs, flag evidence, and critical escalation
- enable only after explicit approval and reconcile the first representative batch

Days 31–60:

- audit invitation coverage for hidden gating or exclusions
- review delivery, opt-outs, conversion, response coverage/time, and recurring themes
- fix customer-experience issues and stale profile content
- validate referrals and downstream conversion
- compare current public profile to the dated top-five baseline without copying peers

Days 61–90:

- refresh the top-five benchmark and category evidence
- evaluate plan value based on actual needs and outcomes
- audit user access, integrations, data retention, and incident history
- update templates, service promises, locations, and owners
- publish an internal quarterly findings/actions report without reviewer PII

## 7. Maintenance and incident triggers

Weekly:

- review/reply queue, SLA, flags, abnormal patterns, and urgent themes
- invitation delivery/failures and suppression

Monthly:

- profile facts, promotions, contacts, categories, locations, and site consistency
- plan limits/usage and analytics baseline
- invitation fairness sample and privacy controls

Quarterly:

- top-five live benchmark
- ownership/access, integrations, retention, and response training
- category fit and product/service evidence

Immediate review triggers:

- Consumer Warning/Alert or hidden TrustScore
- unusual burst or removal of reviews
- invitation sent to an excluded/suppressed cohort
- privacy/security incident
- domain, rebrand, acquisition, closure, or location change
- unsupported method or plan-limit failure
- credentials/access loss
- major product/service incident attracting reviews

Pause only the affected automation when safe, preserve evidence, notify owners, and use official Trustpilot
support. Do not delete records or manipulate reviews to conceal the incident.
