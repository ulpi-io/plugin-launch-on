# Launch on YourStory

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/yourstory/` with `ROUTE-AND-FIT.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `COMPANY-FACTS.md`, `EDITORIAL-PITCH.md`,
`PITCHFEST-PACKET.md`, `DECK-READINESS.md`, `FORM-CAPTURE.md`, `DISCLOSURE.md`, `FOLLOW-UP-RUNBOOK.md`, `analytics.md`,
and shared positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Choose the right channel

Collect sources, story/event, India/market relevance, company facts, stage, founder authority, consented metrics,
deck status, timing, and budget. Browse live routes and return `PROFILE CORRECTION/REQUEST READY`, `EDITORIAL PITCH
READY`, `PITCHFEST APPLICATION READY`, `PAID OPTION TO REVIEW`, or `NOT A FIT`.

### 2. Benchmark exactly five peers

For five comparable company profiles/stories, record sector/tag, headline, product description, factual support,
founder/company context, author/date, narrative arc, source transparency, CTA, and category. Extract structure, not
wording; do not claim that prior coverage proves eligibility.

### 3. Prepare governed content and deck packets

Build a canonical fact sheet, source register, category recommendation, profile blurb, news-led pitch, approved quotes,
media, and correction contact. For PitchFest, prepare only a source-backed deck: problem, solution, customer, market,
business model, traction/funding (where approved), team, ask, and risks—then capture current format and application
fields live.

### 4. Execute with approval and measure

Present exact route, final assets, form values, disclosures, deck, deadlines, and owner for approval. After a profile,
story, or event is live, verify facts, URL, labels, links, category, audience access, and correction path. Track
referrals, qualified conversion, investor/customer follow-up, source accuracy, selection/publication status, and paid
spend separately. End `LIVE — YOURSTORY RUNBOOK ACTIVE`.
