# Launch on OpenHunts

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/openhunts/` with `ROUTE-AND-READINESS.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `LAUNCH-PACKET.md`,
`COMMERCIAL-DECISION.md`, `SCHEDULE.md`, `FORM-CAPTURE.md`, `COMMUNITY-RUNBOOK.md`, `analytics.md`, and positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Select the appropriate launch path

Return `FREE QUEUE READY`, `PREMIUM OPTION TO REVIEW`, `HIGHLIGHT OPTION TO REVIEW`, `SPONSORSHIP OPTION TO REVIEW`,
or `NOT LAUNCH-READY`. Confirm the product works, launch day/time has a support owner, the queue/price remains current,
and expected outcome is discovery and feedback—not guaranteed ranking or promotion.

### 2. Benchmark exactly five peer launches

Use `$browse` to compare exactly five relevant current projects, including any visible Highlight/Sponsored examples.
Capture their factual card patterns, category, vote/rank context, asset/CTA approach, and public conversation. Build a
distinct one-line proposition, category rationale, product proof, and discussion plan.

### 3. Build the submission and community package

Prepare canonical name/domain, concise tagline, customer/problem/solution, category, access/pricing, logo, screenshots
or demo, founder identity, public support URL, launch date/time conversion, approved social/community response owners,
UTMs, and a source register. Write response templates that invite candid feedback, disclose affiliations, and never
ask for manipulated votes.

### 4. Re-capture the account and commercial details

After explicit access approval, record all submission fields/limits and exact free/Premium/Highlight/sponsor terms:
price, currency, tax, queue/slot, schedule, payment/refund, featured duration, carousel/placement, badge condition,
link treatment, and disclosure. Present the selected plan's total cost and tradeoffs in `COMMERCIAL-DECISION.md`.

### 5. Publish with approval and operate

Present form preview, current schedule, field-to-source map, asset licensing, selected plan, and community plan for
approval. After authorized launch, verify the live card, labels, link/UTM paths, timing, and analytics; respond
transparently, correct facts, and measure qualified referrals, feedback, activation, conversion, and spend. End
`LIVE — OPENHUNTS RUNBOOK ACTIVE`.
