# Launch on Startup Grind

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/startup-grind/` with `ROUTE-AND-FIT.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `STARTUP-PACKAGE.md`,
`EVENT-AND-PITCH-PLAN.md`, `COMMUNITY-CONTRIBUTION.md`, `LEAD-AND-PRIVACY.md`, `CHECKLIST.md`, `analytics.md`, and
shared positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Choose the real community route

Ask for product/company, founder owner, audience, local/online chapter, real event/pitch/exhibit target, deadlines,
approved demo, travel/budget, networking objective, follow-up capacity, existing Startup Grind URL, and consent policy.
Browse live surfaces and return `READY TO SUBMIT STARTUP`, `READY FOR EVENT/PITCH`, `READY TO EXHIBIT`, or `NOT A FIT`.

### 2. Benchmark five relevant surfaces

Choose five matching startups, founder discussions, local events, or pitch sessions. Record audience, value exchange,
content style, event logistics, CTA, feedback, and public proof. Do not copy pitches or infer private participation.

### 3. Prepare value-first assets

After authorized form observation, map every live field and prepare official identity, short promise, founder story,
problem/customer/outcome, approved demo, event-specific pitch, one useful discussion contribution, narrow feedback
question, lead-consent language, and follow-up sequence. Keep product claims evidence-backed and stage-appropriate.

### 4. Participate and measure responsibly

Present exact fields, event choice, fees, assets, message/follow-up plan, and owner for approval. Track acceptance,
attendance, meaningful conversations, consented leads, feedback themes, qualified trials, partner opportunities,
community contributions, and cost. End `LIVE — STARTUP GRIND COMMUNITY RUNBOOK ACTIVE`.
