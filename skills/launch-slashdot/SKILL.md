# Launch on Slashdot

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/slashdot/` with `NEWSWORTHINESS.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `SOURCE-PACKET.md`,
`SUBMISSION-PACKET.md`, `COMMENT-RUNBOOK.md`, `CHECKLIST.md`, `analytics.md`, and shared positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Prove newsworthiness

Ask for the event, date, source URLs, why a technical reader cares, availability, technical evidence, relationship to
the submitter, security/embargo review, spokesperson, and approved claims. Browse the live Submit Story route and
return `READY FOR EDITORIAL SUBMISSION` or `NOT NEWSWORTHY FOR SLASHDOT`. Do not turn the latter into a generic launch
plan.

### 2. Benchmark five current stories

Select five comparable stories in the relevant current topic—Software, Open Source, Build, Technology, Devices,
Science, or YRO. Build an evidence table for headline mechanics, source type, factual core, reader debate, comments,
and unsupported-claim risks. Do not copy headlines or narrative.

### 3. Assemble an editor-ready submission

After authorized login, capture every actual field and validation in order. Prepare source-first headline options,
one neutral summary, primary/secondary sources, date/version, technical facts, quote provenance, relationship
disclosure, relevant topic, and anticipated factual questions. Keep the pitch concise, verifiable, and source-linked.
Provide a separate public-response sheet with confirmed facts, corrections path, and issues that need an owner.

### 4. Submit and participate responsibly

Present the exact field table and source packet for approval. After approval, submit once; acceptance and timing are
editorial decisions. If discussed, reply only from an identified authorized account, answer substance with citations,
acknowledge uncertainty, correct errors, and do not debate moderation. Track referral sessions, technical-reader
activation, qualitative feedback, corrections, and incident load. End `LIVE — EDITORIAL DISCUSSION OPERATING`.
