# Launch on BetaList

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/betalist/` with `ROUTE.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `LISTING-PACKAGE.md`, `EARLY-ACCESS-FLOW.md`,
`MEDIA-PLAN.md`, `PLACEMENT-EVALUATION.md`, `CHECKLIST.md`, `analytics.md`, and shared positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Establish eligibility and current submission requirements

Ask for product URL/name, product state, customer/problem/outcome, beta access model, landing-page conversion event,
approved screenshots/logo/video, pricing, privacy/support contact, launch date, capacity, existing BetaList URL, and
budget. Browse the live route. Return `READY TO SUBMIT`, `READY TO CLAIM/UPDATE`, or `BLOCKED — EVIDENCE OR ACCOUNT
NEEDED`.

### 2. Research five live peers

Pick five closest listings by buyer, product type, maturity, category, and access model. Capture the observed field
pattern and separate `LIVE`, `PAID LABEL`, `EDITORIAL`, `INFERRED`, and `UNKNOWN`. Derive positioning whitespace and
conversion objections without copying competitors.

### 3. Prepare the listing fields and early-access funnel

After authorized sign-in, capture every exact required form field, limit, category/tag option, URL rule, asset rule,
review behavior, and paid upsell. Create a field-ready table: official name/domain, precise short description,
expanded description, category/tags, launch stage, access/pricing disclosure, founder/company links, support contact,
logo/screenshots/captions, destination CTA, and UTM URL. Include confirmation, waitlist, invite, onboarding, privacy,
and feedback flow so the listing promise matches the user experience.

### 4. Submit with approval and measure

Present all fields, assets, destination, tracking links, placement choice, and timing for approval before any change.
Track approval status, listing visibility, sessions, signups, confirmed users, activation, feedback, unsubscribes,
support load, paid spend, and cohort retention. Respond honestly to early users and keep content current. End
`LIVE — BETALIST LAUNCH OPERATING`.
