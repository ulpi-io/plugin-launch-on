# Launch on BetaTesting

## Goal

Create `.ulpi/launch/betatesting/` with `FIT-AND-PROJECT-BRIEF.md`, `BENCHMARK.md`, `TEST-DESIGN.md`,
`PARTICIPANT-PLAN.md`, `TASK-AND-SCREENER-PACKET.md`, `DATA-AND-CONSENT.md`, `ACCOUNT-FORM-CAPTURE.md`,
`OPERATIONS-RUNBOOK.md`, `analytics.md`, and positioning.

## Workflow

### 1. Confirm testing fit and test type

Return `PROJECT DESIGN READY`, `DISCOVERY/COMMERCIAL REVIEW NEEDED`, `NOT A TESTING FIT`, or `ACCOUNT CAPTURE
NEEDED`. Select a method only from a real goal: single-session usability feedback, multi-day longitudinal testing,
global manual QA, bug hunt, live/load group, in-home usage, survey, interview, AI-human feedback, gaming, fintech, or
another observed current option. Confirm a stable build/access route, support capacity, participant safety, data
handling, and a product decision that the results can influence.

### 2. Benchmark exactly five live case studies

Use `$browse` to inspect exactly five current public case studies that resemble the product, audience, or method.
Extract the observed objective, participants, test model, timing, assets/logistics, feedback/outcome measures, and
constraints. Build a differentiated plan grounded in the product’s actual risk; use no opaque benchmark result as a
forecast.

### 3. Build the study packet

Write a hypothesis, product/build scope, target participant profile, inclusion/exclusion and screener questions,
country/language/device requirements, target sample range, tasks/scenarios, preconditions, test access, telemetry,
moderator/support procedure, safety/incident process, consent/privacy notice, incentive, schedule, metrics, and
decision rule. Ensure tasks do not ask for credentials, card data, protected health information, secrets, or public
ratings. Define durable artifacts for bug reports, severity, reproducibility, feedback themes, usability, activation,
task success, retention, and withdrawal/deletion requests.

### 4. Capture the live commercial and account workflow

After authorization, choose the `Company` route and record the exact account fields, current plan/pricing/currency,
contract, participant/recruiting controls, screener logic, quotas, country/device targeting, incentive mechanism,
project fields, asset/build permissions, launch/review control, support tier, security/privacy terms, and export or
retention controls. Reconcile every proposed scope and cost against current terms before recommending purchase.

### 5. Launch responsibly and close the loop

Present the scope, field map, project preview, participant risk assessment, budget, timeline, and decision criteria
for approval. After authorized launch, monitor access, support, adverse events, fraud/quality signals, consent,
withdrawals, quota balance, and data minimization. Triage defects and feedback without cherry-picking, issue
incentives as promised, delete/retain data according to approved policy, publish no testimonial without separate
permission, and document product decisions. End `COMPLETE — BETATESTING RESEARCH RUNBOOK CLOSED`.
