## How G2 success works

G2's software score has two relative components:

### Satisfaction

Important inputs include:

- Ease of Use, Meets Requirements, and Quality of Support
- Ease of Admin, Ease of Setup, and Ease of Doing Business With
- Likelihood to Recommend and Direction of Product
- review volume and statistical significance
- review recency
- review completeness/readability
- review source and current-user experience

The score is normalized inside each category, so the same raw inputs can produce different results in
different categories.

### Market Presence

Inputs include product and vendor signals such as:

- employee count and company size
- review volume and recency
- web presence and traffic
- social presence
- growth
- vendor age
- employee satisfaction/engagement
- geographic and category-specific visibility

Product-level inputs receive more weight than vendor-level inputs. Do not manufacture signals or chase vanity
followers; keep public company/product data accurate and build genuine market demand.

### Review decay

G2 recalculates review weight as reviews age. Current methodology says decay starts immediately, remains
gradual for the first 90 days, preserves stronger weight for the first 18 months, and accelerates after that.
Use an always-on, representative review program rather than a one-time burst.

## Current thresholds

Treat these as planning thresholds, not guaranteed outcomes:

| Outcome | Current official threshold or condition |
|---|---|
| Live Grid category | At least 3 products with 10+ reviews |
| Quarterly Grid report category | At least 6 products with 10+ reviews and 150+ reviews total |
| Product inclusion in category Grid | At least 10 reviews attributed to that category |
| Index report category | 150+ reviews total; 6 products with 10+ reviews and enough question responses |
| Product inclusion in Index | At least 10 reviews |
| Public feature ratings | At least 10 responses to feature-related questions |
| Overall Review Sentiment panel | At least 10 responses to a corresponding review question |
| Users Love Us badge | 20 reviews with an average rating of 4.0+ |
| All-in-One/Best-of-Breed classification | 25+ reviews; assigned algorithmically |

Category attribution matters. Ten total product reviews do not necessarily mean ten reviews count in every
category.

Free profiles can qualify for G2 reports and Best Software recognition. Under current documentation, free
profiles can access/share Users Love Us, while downloading and sharing newly earned report badges generally
requires an upgraded profile. Verify current entitlements before using any badge or report content.

## 30/60/90-day plan

### Days 0–7: eligibility and ownership

- finish duplicate search and eligibility verdict
- submit or claim the correct product profile
- assign my.G2 admins and backup owner
- submit category evidence
- create UTM convention and analytics baseline

### Days 8–30: buyer-ready profile

- complete all available profile, pricing, feature, integration, ethics, and media fields
- publish current product screenshots and walkthrough
- verify every CTA and UTM
- create neutral review campaign and invite first representative cohort
- configure review notifications and response SLA
- record baseline profile traffic, reviews, category position, referrals, and conversions

### Days 31–60: evidence and learning

- resolve rejected reviews only through legitimate process guidance
- respond to every published review
- analyze recurring strengths, weaknesses, missing features, and buyer questions
- update public docs or onboarding where reviews expose avoidable confusion
- review category attribution and request a valid audit if evidence shows an error
- compare conversion by profile tab and CTA

### Days 61–90: durable engine

- move from launch burst to always-on, neutral review collection
- refresh weak screenshots, copy, pricing, and proof
- verify official quarterly report deadlines
- prepare lawful badge/report usage if earned and permitted
- route Buyer Intent or Leads into sales only when available on the user's plan
- publish an internal quarterly business review for G2

## Metrics

### Listing health

- submission date, conditional approval, profile approval, public visibility, category assignment, direct URL,
  claim/admin status, and owner
- category request and acceptance status
- percentage of available profile fields complete
- number and age of assets
- broken or redirected URLs
- pricing/docs/profile consistency

### Review health

- eligible users invited
- invite-to-submit and submit-to-approval rates
- approved, pending, rejected, and updated reviews
- review recency distribution
- segment, role, geography, and use-case representation
- category attribution
- average response time and unanswered reviews
- recurring product themes

Do not set an employee performance target based on star rating or positive-review percentage.

### Buyer and business outcomes

- G2 profile and category page views
- CTA clicks by content parameter
- referral sessions and engaged visits
- trial/demo/signup conversions
- assisted pipeline and qualified leads
- influenced opportunities and revenue
- branded and comparison-search visibility

Plan-gated Buyer Intent and Leads should be measured only if actually available.

## Quarterly maintenance

Every quarter:

1. Recheck eligibility, product identity, and duplicate/legacy profiles.
2. Review every category and its current inclusion criteria.
3. Refresh overview, pricing, packages, features, integrations, languages, deployment, security, and AI facts.
4. Replace stale screenshots and video.
5. Test all links and UTMs.
6. Audit review recency, audience representation, moderation, and response SLA.
7. Read every negative theme and assign a product/customer-success action.
8. Check report deadlines, thresholds, badge eligibility, and content-use rights.
9. Review competitor/comparison accuracy without manipulating it.
10. Report referrals, conversions, influenced pipeline, and next-quarter experiments.

## Failure modes

- Listing a B2C or beta product and wasting the research review.
- Submitting a duplicate instead of claiming an existing profile.
- Choosing categories by aspiration or competitor presence.
- Treating the profile as a backlink instead of a buyer conversion page.
- Hiding pricing without explaining the quote path.
- Running one review burst and allowing the base to decay.
- Gating reviews or asking only promoters.
- Responding defensively to criticism.
- Claiming badges, ranks, report data, or logo rights outside the current plan/license.
- Optimizing total reviews while ignoring category attribution and buyer outcomes.
